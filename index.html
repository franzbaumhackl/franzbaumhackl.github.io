Mechanical Arm
University Rover Competition (In Progress)
SolidWorks Mechanisms Loads Integration
Objective
  • Design a rigid, dexterous, and robust robotic arm capable of completing delivery and equipment servicing missions onboard a Mars rover.
  • Delivery mission: manipulate objects while traversing difficult terrain; open hinged box and relocate objects no more than 5 kg and no larger than 40 x 40 x 40 cm of varying shape.
  • Equipment servicing mission: perform dexterous operations requiring a high degree of skill, control, and coordination.
Constraints
  • Arm must be easily assembled and removable during competition time.
  • Hand must hold and manipulate a 5 kg object.
  • Must fit within a 1.2 x 1.2 x 1.2 m box.
  • Rover maximum weight 50 kg; total mass of all fielded rover parts for all events 70 kg.
  • Cannot fully enclose electronics within arm.
Approach
  • Delegated subsystem CAD tasks and provided in-person design support to ensure progress and skill development across the team.
  • Selected truss-like structural members to reduce mass and improve load paths; emphasized parametric CAD for rapid iteration.
  • Estimated joint torques using a robotic arm torque calculator reference and early load cases.

Cube Satellite
CubeSat Launch Initiative (Complete)
Thermal Desktop ANSYS SolidWorks Test Fixtures
Objective
  • Design and modify CubeSat structure to mount flight hardware; design test fixtures for vibration testing of batteries on an electromagnetic shaker.
  • Analyze thermal margins of electrical hardware and structural components; finalize material properties, outgassing, and bill of materials.
  • Support mission goal: REALOP tests feasibility of hard disk drives as alternatives to reaction wheels for attitude control, downlinking accelerometer data to a ground station.
Constraints
  • Structure compatible with 10 x 10 x 20 cm form factor.
  • Total mass below 2 kg.
  • Thermal limits: circuit boards below 100 °C; batteries below 60 °C.
  • Outgassing limits: total mass loss < 1%, CVCM < 0.1%; part volume above minimum volume requirement (mass/density).
